## 2. RobosizeME — Diagnosed Pain Points

### Pain Point 1: Seed-to-Series A Revenue Pressure
- SeedTwo Capital's investment thesis focuses on companies that can demonstrate a **repeatable revenue engine** to justify a Series A
- With $2M raised, RobosizeME has an 18–24 month window to prove predictable pipeline, consistent close rates, and growing ARR
- Without a structured sales system, this proof is nearly impossible to produce at the pace investors expect

### Pain Point 2: Founder-Led Sales Ceiling
- No evidence of a dedicated sales team or CRO/VP Sales hire yet (as of April 2026)
- Founders selling is the default at this stage — it works early but creates a single point of failure and doesn't scale
- Roger Sholanki (Book4Time founder, now SeedTwo Capital) experienced this exact pattern himself: *"I was doing a lot of the day-to-day operations of the business"* — and had to be taught to step back

### Pain Point 3: High False Hire Risk
- First sales hires at seed-stage companies typically fail at a 60%+ rate without structured screening
- Roger Sholanki explicitly stated: *"I must have gone through half a dozen false hires. We took out millions of dollars in lines of credit that were being invested in new sales hires and we weren't getting the results we wanted."*
- This is the exact moment RobosizeME is entering — the first "real" sales hire window post-seed

### Pain Point 4: No Documented Sales Playbook
- Multi-geography presence (EU, NA, MEA, APAC) with no evidence of a documented, repeatable playbook
- Without training infrastructure, each new rep reinvents discovery, objection handling, and the ROI business case — leading to longer ramp times and inconsistent deal outcomes
- Hospitality tech sales is a niche, complex buyer environment requiring specific product + industry knowledge

### Pain Point 5: Consultative ROI Selling Required
- RobosizeME's value proposition is entirely ROI-driven (labor savings, cost recovery, headcount reduction)
- This is not a features-based sell — it requires reps to lead with deep operational discovery, quantify pain, and co-build a business case with the buyer
- *"When you're selling enterprise software, it's really doing a deep discovery"* — Roger Sholanki
- Generic SaaS reps default to feature pitching; this is a learned skill that requires specific training

### Pain Point 6: Tight Hospitality Community = Churn Risk
- Hotel tech is a small, relationship-driven industry — a bad rep or a churned customer creates reputational damage that compounds
- Sean Anderson (Sales Academy founder, formerly at Book4Time): *"It was such a small world, such a small community. Word travels fast. You can't have sellers that are just going out there to get that initial one-year deal."*
- Every hire must be customer-first, not commission-first

### Pain Point 7: Pipeline Predictability
- Roger Sholanki's stated #1 challenge at Book4Time: *"Having a predictable sales pipeline is so challenging for a lot of founders. Your sales reps are telling you 'we're going to close this next month' and that doesn't happen."*
- Without proper CRM hygiene, mutual action plans, and forecast discipline, RobosizeME's pipeline data will be unreliable — a direct problem for investor reporting

## 4. Sales Academy — Methodology & Services (From Transcripts)

### 4a. The Hiring Framework: DNA-PRO
Named and described by Jamie Smithers (CRO, Traction Complete) — a Sales Academy network contact — in the podcast transcripts:

| Letter | Attribute | What to Look For |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **D** | Drive | Internal motivation, competitive hunger, desire to win — cannot be trained in |
| **N** | Nature | How they show up; personal brand; buyers buy from people they trust |
| **A** | Acumen | Quality of questions they ask; business curiosity; strategic thinking |
| **P** | Performance | What have they accomplished — anywhere, not just in sales |
| **R** | Relevance | SaaS background, industry familiarity, tech-stack comfort |
| **O** | Opportunities | Career trajectory signals — why they made the moves they made |

### 4b. Psychometric Assessment System
- Sales Academy uses a **probability-scoring assessment** to screen candidates
- Tests for: background, motivations, drive indicators, behavioral traits
- Output: a numerical probability score for on-the-job success
- Roger Sholanki: *"He brought in assessments in terms of testing that we would do on individuals to understand their background, what motivates somebody, what is their drive... it provided a scoring of probability on somebody succeeding. We used that religiously in every hire."*

### 4c. Training Methodology: Customer-Centric Selling
- The named methodology Sales Academy uses and teaches: **Customer-Centric Selling (CCS)**
- Core principle: lead with deep discovery — understand the buyer's operational pain before presenting any solution
- Delivery format:
  - Group training sessions (founder + all reps together)
  - Individual 1:1 coaching sessions
  - Ongoing call reviews and pipeline inspection
- Roger Sholanki: *"Sean spent a lot of time doing deep discovery training sessions in groups and individually with folks to educate them on our product and then having one-on-ones with them to coach them."*

### 4d. Onboarding System
- Structured, staged onboarding — not ad-hoc
- Standardization across reps enables scalability as team grows
- Jamie Smithers (Traction Complete): *"If you don't have that solid foundation, you're going to have problems downstream as that company continues to grow and evolve."*
- Built to capture "A-player" behaviors so tribal knowledge is transferable to new hires

### 4e. Pipeline & Forecast Discipline
- Installs mutual action plans (MAPs), executive sponsor frameworks, competitive positioning
- Establishes ICP-based territory focus (not "spray and pray" BD)
- Jamie Smithers on joining a new company: *"Have you guys heard of a mutual action plan? Have you worked with executive sponsors? Who's our competition? Do we have a unique point of view on how we win?"*
- Delivers forecast accuracy — critical for investor-facing pipeline reporting

### 4f. Rep Performance Management
- Explicit guidance on when to cut underperforming reps
- Roger Sholanki: *"You don't want to hold on to reps in hopes that they're going to succeed one day. If they haven't succeeded in a short period... that person's not going to work out. Being quick on pulling the trigger when things aren't working out is important."*

## 5. The Book4Time Case Study — Full Detail

This is the cornerstone proof point for the pitch. All quotes are Roger Sholanki's own words from the YouTube transcript (SalesAcademy3-3.txt).

### Context
- Book4Time: hospitality SaaS for spa and wellness operations at major hotel chains
- Used in 100+ countries, major brand hotels

- Eventually exited for ~$200M

### The Pain (Before Sales Academy)
- *"Our challenge was being able to have predictable forecasting."*
- *"I must have gone through probably half a dozen false hires."*
- *"We took out millions of dollars in lines of credit that were being invested in new sales hires and we weren't getting the results we wanted."*
- *"I was doing a lot of the hand-to-hand day-to-day operations of the business."*

- Sent a **FedEx package** containing two fuzzy dice with a note: *"Don't gamble on your next sales hire — Sales Academy"*
- Roger ignored calls for weeks — the physical mail broke through
- When they met: Sean asked 100 deep questions, did not pitch. Roger reflected: *"He must have asked me a hundred different questions, very deep questions... Got me to really think how I was running the business."*
- Roger called Sean after that meeting and brought him on board

### What Sales Academy Implemented
1. Customer-Centric Selling training (group + individual)
2. Deep discovery coaching sessions
3. Psychometric assessment system for all new hires
4. DNA screening to identify the right "sales DNA" for hospitality enterprise
5. Pipeline discipline, forecast accuracy, rep performance benchmarks

### The Outcome
- *"We started putting some of those in place and we started seeing results."*
- *"By the time we sold the business, historically over the past 5 years, we had consistent sales growth."*
- *"Sean and Sales Academy were really valuable to our valuation."*
- **Direct attribution to investor attraction:** *"I think what Sean was doing in sales was one of the top things that attracted Serent Capital. If you have a consistent, predictable sales pipeline and results, that's going to add value."*

## 11. Report Generation Guidance

When generating the final pitch report, the LLM should:

1. **Lead with RobosizeME's world** — their seed raise, investor thesis, timeline pressure — before introducing Sales Academy
2. **Use Roger's story as the structural spine** — his journey from false hires to predictable pipeline to exit is the proof narrative
3. **Name the methodology explicitly** — Customer-Centric Selling, DNA-PRO framework, psychometric scoring — not vague promises
4. **Quantify everything possible** — €4M labor recovery, $1M saved, 5 years consistent growth, half-dozen false hires, millions in wasted credit lines
5. **Acknowledge the niche** — hospitality enterprise sales is different; Sean has done it; generic sales trainers haven't
6. **Close with the Roger bridge** — he's in the room. He's already a believer. The pitch is asking RobosizeME to do what their own investor already knows works.
7. **Tone:** Confident, peer-level, consultative — not vendor-y. Sean's own approach (100 questions, no pitch) should be reflected in the report's framing
